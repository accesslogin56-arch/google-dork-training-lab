What Not To Do

Google dorks are search queries. They can be useful for learning, research, and checking your own site — but they can also lead to exposed systems or private information if used carelessly.

Important: Just because Google can find something does not mean you are allowed to access, interact with, copy, save, test, or use it.

Do not use dorks to access real exposed systems

  • Do not open or interact with real camera feeds.
  • Do not try to log in to real admin panels.
  • Do not download private backups, logs, config files, or databases.
  • Do not test passwords, tokens, API keys, or credentials you find online.
  • Do not probe, scan, exploit, or interfere with systems you do not own.
  • Do not share exposed private data, even if it was publicly indexed.

Why this matters

Search engines may index pages by accident. A misconfigured server, forgotten file, or exposed dashboard can still be private or sensitive. Finding it does not make it yours.

Safe learning means staying inside authorised environments, public information, and deliberately fake training labs like this one.

Safe uses: checking your own website, finding public documents, learning search operators, researching official guidance, and practising inside authorised labs.
