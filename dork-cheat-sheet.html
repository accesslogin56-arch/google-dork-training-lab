Google Dork Cheat Sheet

Google dorks are advanced search queries that use operators to narrow results. This page explains the basics using only this safe demo lab.

Operator What it does Safe example
site: Limits results to one website or domain. site:accesslogin56-arch.github.io/google-dork-training-lab
intitle: Finds pages with a word or phrase in the page title. site:accesslogin56-arch.github.io/google-dork-training-lab intitle:"admin"
inurl: Finds pages where the URL contains a word. site:accesslogin56-arch.github.io/google-dork-training-lab inurl:camera
filetype: Finds a specific type of file, such as PDF or TXT. site:accesslogin56-arch.github.io/google-dork-training-lab filetype:pdf
"exact phrase" Finds pages containing the exact phrase inside quotation marks. site:accesslogin56-arch.github.io/google-dork-training-lab "FAKE TRAINING DATA ONLY"
-word Excludes results containing a word. site:accesslogin56-arch.github.io/google-dork-training-lab -camera
OR Searches for either one term or another. site:accesslogin56-arch.github.io/google-dork-training-lab admin OR camera
Safety note: Only use dorks against your own sites, intentionally vulnerable labs, public documents, or systems where you have permission. Google indexing something does not mean it is fair game.

Good uses

  • Finding public PDFs and reports
  • Checking what your own website exposes
  • Researching official guidance and documents
  • Learning how search indexing works
